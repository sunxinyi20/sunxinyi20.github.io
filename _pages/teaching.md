## Teaching Philosophy

Marketing teaching should connect **theory, data, and managerial decision-making**. My goal is for students to (a) organize their thinking using theoretical frameworks, (b) test and calibrate their judgments with data, and (c) make defensible business decisions.

I take a **problem-first approach**: define the business problem first, then select the appropriate methodological tool, and finally explain what the method reveals — and what it cannot. My research — spanning TikTok Shop, REDnote, university forums, and e-commerce platforms — provides real-world cases that I bring into the classroom.

I design for diverse student backgrounds. For technical students, I provide formal derivations and coding exercises. For non-technical students, I offer intuitive explanations and visual diagnostics. As an international student myself, I understand the barriers that language and cultural gaps can create in class participation, and I use structured small-group discussions and written prompts to ensure every student can engage.
